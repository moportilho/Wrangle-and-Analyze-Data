Report: act_report¶

  • Create a 250-word-minimum written report called "act_report.pdf" or "act_report.html" that communicates the insights and displays the visualization(s) produced from your wrangled data. This is to be framed as an external document, like a blog post or magazine article, for example.

WeRateDogs is a popular Twitter account that rates adorable dogs with humorous captions. As a data enthusiast, I embarked on a data wrangling journey to gather, assess, and clean data from various sources to understand the correlation between retweet count and favorite count for these dog ratings. The wrangling process involved handling missing data, data type conversions, and merging datasets to create a comprehensive and tidy master DataFrame.

Insight 1 - Positive Correlation:

After analyzing the cleaned dataset, I found a strong positive correlation between retweet count and favorite count for WeRateDogs tweets. The scatter plot displayed a clear upward trend, indicating that tweets with higher retweet counts tend to have higher favorite counts as well. This observation suggests that the more people retweet a dog rating, the more likely it is to receive more favorites, likely due to the tweet's popularity and appeal.

Insight 2 - Popular Dog Breeds:

I delved deeper into the data to explore the relationship between retweet count, favorite count, and dog breeds. The analysis revealed certain dog breeds that consistently received high retweet and favorite counts. The Golden Retriever, Labrador Retriever, and Pembroke were among the top-rated and most popular breeds on WeRateDogs. This insight provides valuable information for dog enthusiasts and pet owners interested in understanding the breeds that captivate the Twitter community.

Insight 3 - Engagement Over Time:

To understand if there were any trends in user engagement, I investigated the relationship between retweet count, favorite count, and timestamp. Surprisingly, there was a noticeable increase in user engagement over time, with tweets posted in recent months receiving higher retweet and favorite counts compared to earlier tweets. This suggests that WeRateDogs' popularity has grown over time, and the community has become more active in sharing and favoriting dog ratings.

In conclusion, the data exploration and analysis of WeRateDogs tweets revealed a positive correlation between retweet count and favorite count, indicating that tweets with higher retweet counts tend to be more popular and receive more favorites. Additionally, certain dog breeds stood out as favorites among the Twitter community, while engagement with WeRateDogs has grown over time. These insights provide a glimpse into the captivating world of adorable dogs and the Twitter community's affection for them. Althought, the data analysis is based on a limited dataset, and further research and statistical analysis may be required for a more comprehensive understanding of the correlations and trends observed.
